What is Climate Change?

Most people by now have heard of the term climate change but might not necessarily know the real meaning of it. Climate Change, as the name suggests has to do with the changes in humidity and temperatures of a particular area. Due to human actions, these once-natural factors are now posing a threat to earth's natural ecosystems. Animals and the landscapes that surround them are negatively impacted by climate change. As such, global warming is one causation of this man-made phenomenom. Discover more and make a difference.

Reality of Climate Change

Currently, the world is warming faster now compared to any part of documented history. Factors that directly contribute to this are things like generating electricity and heat. Burning fossil fuels such as coal, oil, and natural gas produce heat-trapping gasses that create molecules in the atmosphere that absorb light. This as a result forms a 'blanket' of gases around Earth. As a result, heat is unable to escape. Gases that are responsible for this include carbon dioxide, methane, nitrous oxide, and water vapor.

Climate Change has real life consequences. Due to the increased heat in the atmosphere, polar ice caps are melting at a rate of almost 13% per decade. Moreover, the oldest ice in the Artic has declined by an overwhelming 95%. It is important to note that this drastically increases the sea level on Earth. If Climate Change is not stopped, coastal cities will cease to exist and the ecosystems that many animals rely on (including us humans) will collapse.

TOP FIVE Companies that are making a DIFFERENCE


Ranks & Contributions
#1 Alphabet Company:
Alphabet is the world’s largest corporate buyer of renewable energy, which includes more than 50 projects totaling 5.5 GW of renewable energy projects under contract worldwide. This accounts for an annual deficit of approximately five million tons of carbon dioxide in the atmosphere.
#2 Beyond Meat Company
A peer-reviewed Life Cycle Analysis from the University of Michigan assessed the Beyond Burger’s environmental impact compared to a standard quarter pounder. The study determined that the Beyond Burger required 99% less land, 93% less land, 46% less energy, and produced 90% fewer greenhouse gas emissions compared to its cow counterpart.
#3 Hewlett Packard Company
Information technology giant HP is making fighting climate change a primary goal in the coming decades. The company has committed to eliminating 75% of its single-use plastic packaging by 2025 compared to 2018 levels.
#4 Unilever Company
Unilever is an industry leader in reusable and recyclable packaging, thanks in part to a partnership with Loop where single-use products are sold in refillable glass and stainless steel packaging that consumers can continue to reuse.
#5 Johnson&Johnson
As the parent company of skincare brands Johnson’s, Neutrogena, and Aveeno, Johnson & Johnson plans to do its part to combat climate change by committing to packaging that is 100% recyclable, reusable, or compostable by 2025. And while its facilities are currently powered by 35% renewable energy, it plans to bring this percentage to 100% by 2050.
