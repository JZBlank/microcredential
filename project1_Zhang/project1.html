What is Climate Change?

Most people by now have heard of the term climate change but might not necessarily know the real meaning of it. Climate Change, as the name suggests has to do with the changes in humidity and temperatures of a particular area. Due to human actions, these once-natural factors are now posing a threat to earth's natural ecosystems. Animals and the landscapes that surround them are negatively impacted by climate change. As such, global warming is one causation of this man-made phenomenom.

Discover the reason behind Climate Change

Currently, the world is warming faster now compared to any part of documented history. Factors that directly contribute to this are things like generating electricity and heat. Burning fossil fuels such as coal, oil, and natural gas produce heat-trapping gasses that create molecules in the atmosphere that absorb light. This as a result forms a 'blanket' of gases around Earth. As a result, heat is unable to escape. Gases that are responsible for this include carbon dioxide, methane, nitrous oxide, and water vapor.
